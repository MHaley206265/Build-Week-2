Pulsars: Universal Time-Keepers

Pulsars are an important aspect of the universe that we live in. Pulsars are stars that rotate and shoot beams of powerful radiation from their poles. The reason they are called pulsars is because it looks as though the light coming from them is pulsing, or flashing, really fast. The reason they are important to scientists is because their period, or the duration and spacing of their pulse, is almost constant. This means that they can be used as a measurement of time and, in the future, could also be used as important landmarks when mapping areas of space.

Finding Pulsars

One of the things that makes pulsars useful as a landmark is that they are somewhat rare. Finding them can be a challenge and usually involves looking over tons of data collected from telescopes in order to determine whether one of the twinkling dots in the sky happens to be one of these elusive treasures. In the past, this often meant a lot of man-hours were poured into scouring through this data in order to discover the existance of a pulsar. Now, it is possible using machine learning algorithms to determine with a high degree of accuracy whether or not the data is representative of a pulsar or not.
